Q1: When should I call a locksmith?
Answer: Whenever you feel the need to change your locks around the house. If it is a new place that you just moved into, upgrading the security or just because you feel there might be a problem with the lock. And of course whenever you are locked in/out.
Q2: How long should I wait to the locksmith?
Answer: Most locksmiths”guarantee” a 30 minutes response time, which usually that is the case, but you need to understand that sometimes there are unpredictable delays like traffic and such so please be patient but if you feel that it might be too long (over 45 minutes wait) please look for another locksmith but make sure to tell the locksmith that is on his way in advance.
Q3: How much should I pay?
Answer: Depends on the type of job you need and the products you choose. Most locksmiths charge around 60 GBP for a lockout. But it may be more if you chose an expensive lock so make sure you look online before paying (just remember there is a price difference between online and on emergency).
Q4: How do I know that the lock I’m being offered is the right lock for me?
Answer: Simple, look at the old lock and look at the new look you are being offered and see if the shape is similar, then look at the upgrades or differences but always make sure you’ll have a British Standard kite mark for the insurance purposes.
Q5: How long does it take to fix a lock or open a locked door?
Answer: There is no minimum or maximum time. Opening a door can take 4 seconds or 1 hour or even more, depends on the lock type and the mechanism, just makes sure that the locksmith is explaining what he is going to do before he starts to work on your door.
